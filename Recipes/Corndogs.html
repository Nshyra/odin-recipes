CornDogs

Description

A sausage (usaually a hot dog) that was been skewered, covered in batter, and then deep fried. An American staple that may have been created by german ummigrants in Texas. Many variations of the corndog like a breakfast corndogs or korean corndogs just to name a few.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • 1 Cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 Cup white sugar
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 quart vegetable oil
  • 2 packs (16 oz.) packs of beef franks
  • 16 wooden skewers
    •

    Steps

    1. combine cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl then stir milk and egg to make the batter.

    2. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large saucepan to 375 degrees(F). While heating oil pat dry the franks and insert a skewer into each one. Then roll franks in the batter until well coated.

    3. Fry 2 or 3 corndogs at a time in the preheated oil until lighlty browned, about 3 minutes. Drain on paper towels after taking them out.
